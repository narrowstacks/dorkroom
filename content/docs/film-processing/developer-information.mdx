## Popular Black and White Developers

### Kodak D-76

- **Type:** Fine-grain powder developer
- **Dilution:** Stock or 1:1 for fine grain, 1:2 for sharper results
- **Development Time:** 6–12 minutes depending on film
- **Temperature:** 20 °C (68 °F)
- **Agitation:** 10 seconds every 30 seconds
- **Best For:** All-purpose use with consistent tonality
- **Notes:** Economical; stock solution can be reused

### Ilford ID-11

- **Type:** Fine-grain powder developer
- **Dilution:** Stock, 1:1, or 1:3 for acutance
- **Development Time:** 7–14 minutes
- **Temperature:** 20 °C (68 °F)
- **Agitation:** 10 seconds every 30 seconds
- **Best For:** Ilford emulsions and general purpose
- **Notes:** Slightly cleaner working than D-76

### Kodak HC-110

- **Type:** Liquid concentrate developer
- **Dilution:** B (1:31) is most common; A (1:15), C (1:47), D (1:63)
- **Development Time:** 5–10 minutes depending on dilution
- **Temperature:** 20 °C (68 °F)
- **Agitation:** 5 seconds every 30 seconds
- **Best For:** Convenience, long shelf life, consistent results
- **Notes:** Syrupy concentrate is easy to measure precisely

### Ilford Perceptol

- **Type:** Ultra fine-grain, compensating developer
- **Dilution:** Stock or 1:3
- **Development Time:** 8–16 minutes
- **Temperature:** 20 °C (68 °F)
- **Agitation:** 5 seconds every minute (minimal agitation)
- **Best For:** High-contrast scenes, smooth grain, reduced speed
- **Notes:** Expect 1/3 to 1 stop speed loss

### Rodinal (Adonal)

- **Type:** High-acutance developer
- **Dilution:** 1:25 standard, 1:50+ for longer times
- **Development Time:** 8–14 minutes
- **Temperature:** 20 °C (68 °F)
- **Agitation:** 10 seconds every 30 seconds
- **Best For:** Maximum sharpness, architecture, landscapes
- **Notes:** Extremely long shelf life; signature crisp grain

### Kodak X-Tol

- **Type:** Fine-grain, ascorbic-acid developer
- **Dilution:** Stock or 1:1 for fine grain, 1:2 for normal use
- **Development Time:** 6–11 minutes
- **Temperature:** 20 °C (68 °F)
- **Agitation:** 10 seconds every 30 seconds
- **Best For:** Excellent sharpness with low toxicity
- **Notes:** Watch for sudden failure when mixed improperly

## Special-Purpose Developers

### Kodak Microdol-X

- **Type:** Ultra fine-grain developer
- **Dilution:** Stock or 1:3
- **Development Time:** 9–15 minutes
- **Agitation:** 5 seconds every minute
- **Best For:** Portraits, product work, high enlargements
- **Notes:** Reduces film speed by roughly one stop

### Pyrocat-HD

- **Type:** Staining developer
- **Dilution:** 1:1:100 standard working solution
- **Development Time:** 8–12 minutes
- **Temperature:** 21 °C (70 °F)
- **Agitation:** Minimal inversion or rotary processing
- **Best For:** Large format negatives and alt processes
- **Notes:** Stain masks grain and boosts highlight separation

## Developer Shelf Life

- Stock solutions: ~6 months sealed, 2 months opened (most powders)
- Working solutions: 24 hours (D-76 1:1), 1–2 hours (Perceptol), ~5 days (Rodinal)
- HC-110 concentrate: virtually indefinite
- X-Tol: ~6 months sealed; discard at first sign of failure

## Mixing Best Practices

- Use distilled water for sensitive developers
- Dissolve Part A completely before adding Part B
- Store in amber glass or accordion bottles
- Label each bottle with mix date and dilution

## Safety and Handling

- Wear gloves and ensure proper ventilation
- Label every container clearly
- Dispose of spent chemicals according to local regulations
- Store chemistry away from children and pets
