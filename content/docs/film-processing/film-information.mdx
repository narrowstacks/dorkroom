### Kodak Tri-X 400

- **ISO:** 400 (pushable to 800–1600)
- **Grain:** Medium, classic look
- **Contrast:** Medium-high, excellent tonal range
- **Best Use:** Street photography, photojournalism, low light
- **Development:** Very forgiving with most developers

### Ilford HP5 Plus 400

- **ISO:** 400 (pushable to 800–3200)
- **Grain:** Medium, smooth look
- **Contrast:** Medium, very flexible
- **Best Use:** Versatile general-purpose film
- **Development:** Pairs well with Ilford ID-11 or Microphen

### Ilford FP4 Plus 125

- **ISO:** 125 (pull to 80 for finer grain)
- **Grain:** Fine
- **Contrast:** Neutral
- **Best Use:** Landscapes, portraits, studio work
- **Development:** Works brilliantly with Perceptol for ultra-fine grain

### Kodak T-Max 100

- **ISO:** 100
- **Grain:** Extremely fine T-grain emulsion
- **Contrast:** Neutral to high
- **Best Use:** High-resolution work and large enlargements
- **Development:** Optimised for T-Max developer

### Fomapan 400 Action

- **ISO:** 400 (shoots best at 200–320)
- **Grain:** Pronounced, classic look
- **Contrast:** High
- **Best Use:** Vintage aesthetic or dramatic contrast scenes
- **Development:** Rodinal 1:50 for strong edge sharpness

## Color Negative Films

### Kodak Portra 400

- **Color Balance:** Warm skin tones, natural palette
- **Exposure Latitude:** +3 / –1 stops
- **Best Use:** Portraits, weddings, lifestyle imagery
- **Notes:** Overexpose by 1 stop for soft pastels

### Kodak Ektar 100

- **Color Balance:** Punchy, saturated colors
- **Exposure Latitude:** ±2 stops
- **Best Use:** Landscapes, product, travel
- **Notes:** Meter carefully to avoid clipping reds

### Fujifilm Pro 400H (discontinued)

- **Color Balance:** Cool tones, flattering for skin
- **Exposure Latitude:** +4 / –1 stops
- **Best Use:** Portraits and weddings
- **Notes:** Overexpose by 2 stops for pastel greens

### CineStill 800T

- **Color Balance:** Tungsten-balanced for night scenes
- **Exposure Latitude:** +2 / –1 stops
- **Best Use:** Neon, cityscapes, mixed lighting
- **Notes:** Use an 85B filter for daylight shooting

## Slide Films (E-6)

### Fujifilm Velvia 50

- **Color Balance:** Extremely saturated and contrasty
- **Exposure Latitude:** ±0.5 stops
- **Best Use:** Landscapes, macro, product
- **Notes:** Meter precisely; best on tripod

### Kodak Ektachrome E100

- **Color Balance:** Neutral-cool rendition
- **Exposure Latitude:** ±0.5 stops
- **Best Use:** Editorial and portrait work
- **Notes:** Excellent reciprocity characteristics

## Pushing and Pulling Film

### Push Processing

- Increase development time or temperature for underexposed rolls
- Expect more contrast and grain
- Ideal for low-light or when faster shutter speeds are needed

### Pull Processing

- Reduce development time to compensate for overexposure
- Produces lower contrast and finer grain
- Helpful for taming high-contrast scenes

## Storage and Handling Tips

- Store film in a cool, dry environment
- Refrigerate color film for long-term storage
- Freeze rare or discontinued stocks (let them thaw before use)
- Keep rolls in original packaging to prevent light leaks
